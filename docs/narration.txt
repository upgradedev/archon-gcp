Every small-business owner reads the bank statement and assumes that's their financial picture. It isn't. The real picture only appears when you bring every document together and cross-check them against each other. That's Archon: a unified financial intelligence platform. The new era of financial intelligence.

Archon is built to ingest everything a business generates, sales and purchase invoices, orders, receipts, payments, bank transfers, payroll, expenses, even sales targets, into one environment, and turn it into a consolidated, period-over-period view: profit and loss, EBITDA, cash, and the true cost of your team. It's a Gemini agent, built on Google's Agent Development Kit, running on GCP.

Here it goes through one month. You hand it the documents the business actually received, and it classifies each one and posts a balanced double-entry journal entry. Six documents become six balanced journal entries. The language model orchestrates, but the bookkeeping math is deterministic, so the numbers are auditable.

Then comes the part a dashboard never does: it cross-checks the whole picture for what's missing or inconsistent. Every bank line is reconciled to the document that justifies it. A payment with no matching invoice gets flagged, did the vendor never send it, did the bookkeeper never register it, or is the payment itself wrong? That completeness check is the heart of the product.

It also runs an evaluation gate, four cross-document consistency and reconciliation rules, R1 through R4. The bank payment matches the payroll it settles within two percent. Employer cost over gross sits in the expected band. The payment date is inside the period. And gross covers net plus withheld tax. Four out of four pass, so the whole picture holds together.

And here is one insight among many. Workforce cost this month is twenty-eight thousand two hundred forty-nine euros, the true cost of employing the team. But only fourteen thousand three hundred fifty actually left the bank, to the employees. The other thirteen thousand eight hundred ninety-nine, employer social-security contributions and withheld tax, is a payable that settles later. Profit and cash are different stories, and Archon keeps both straight.

The analysis itself is a Google ADK Sequential Agent, three sub-agents in order, reconciler, validator, narrator, each passing its result forward through shared state. The conversational agent adds four tools and session memory, so you can feed documents across turns and just ask, in plain language.
